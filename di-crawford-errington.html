01 · Positioning

Your Commercial Identity

I'm Di Crawford-Errington, and I've spent 20+ years becoming New Zealand's most trusted name in payroll training. My business, Small Business Training Hub (SBTH), exists because NZ payroll legislation is genuinely one of the most complex payroll systems in the world — and most small business owners are trying to navigate it blind. At SBTH, we give employers, bookkeepers, and payroll practitioners the knowledge and confidence to get payroll right: accurate calculations, full compliance, and no more 3am anxiety about whether the Holidays Act is being applied correctly. Our flagship NZ Payroll Essentials Training Program is registered with the Regional Business Partner Network — meaning eligible businesses can access government-funded vouchers to cover their training. We're not a software company. We're not a generic bookkeeping firm. We are the payroll training specialist for Kiwi business — and the NOW ecosystem is exactly the referral infrastructure that puts SBTH in front of the business owners, advisors, and coaches who see the payroll problem before their clients do.

02 · Ideal Client Profile

Who You Serve

SBTH's perfect-fit clients are NZ small business owners and bookkeepers who process payroll but don't fully understand the legislation behind it. Typically 5–50 employees, across trades, professional services, retail, and hospitality. They're compliant enough to have not been caught yet — but anxious enough to know they're on borrowed time. Bookkeepers and accounting practices are a second powerful ICP: those who want to upskill or offer payroll expertise as a service to their own SME clients. Both ICPs are eligible for RBPN-funded vouchers — making the barrier to enrolment exceptionally low when the referral is made at the right moment.

13 · Risks & Watchouts

Weak Fits to Know

Non-NZ Partners

SBTH's content is built entirely around NZ payroll legislation. Partners focused on Australian or international SME clients won't generate meaningful referrals — the course is not relevant to their audience.

Large Enterprise Focus

SBTH's sweet spot is 5–50 employees. Partners whose clients are enterprise-scale businesses with dedicated payroll departments are not a fit — different complexity, different solution.

Payroll Software Vendors

Software companies often position themselves as the full payroll solution. Some are resistant to acknowledging that their software doesn't solve the legislation knowledge gap — and therefore won't refer to SBTH. Qualify the partnership intent early.

Partners Who Solve Everything In-House

Coaches or advisors who pride themselves on having an answer for everything may not refer specialists. A partner who says "I handle payroll with my clients myself" is unlikely to generate SBTH enrolments.

14 · Reciprocity Engine

How the Engine Works

SBTH receives referrals when partners identify the payroll knowledge gap before the client does — in business coaching sessions, accounting reviews, bookkeeper onboarding, HR consultations, or IT implementations. The RBPN funding angle makes every referral frictionless: "it may even be government-funded" removes the cost objection instantly. SBTH gives referrals when graduates are ready for the next layer of business support: accounting, coaching, HR, digital marketing, finance, and legal. The reciprocity loop is especially clean with bookkeepers (SBTH trains them, they send their own SME clients back) and business coaches (they send payroll-anxious clients to SBTH, SBTH sends newly-confident graduates back for growth strategy). Trust compounds every time a client enrols through a referral and reports back to the partner that the course delivered. Repeat triggers happen every time a business hires another employee, changes pay structures, or faces an IRD review.
